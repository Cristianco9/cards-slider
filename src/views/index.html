THE SOLAR SYSTEM

  • Mercury

    4,879 KM

    The smallest and closest planet to the Sun, with extreme temperatures due to its thin atmosphere.

  • Venus

    12,104 KM

    Similar in size to Earth but with a thick, toxic atmosphere causing a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet.

  • Earth

    12,742 KM

    The only planet known to support life, with water, land, and an atmosphere that sustains living organisms.

  • Mars

    6,779 KM

    Known as the "Red Planet" due to iron oxide on its surface. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the Solar System.

  • Jupiter

    139,8202 KM

    The largest planet, with a massive storm called the "Great Red Spot", is the most older planet, and at least 79 moons.

  • Saturn

    116,460 KM

    Famous for its bright, icy rings and many moons, including Titan, which has lakes of liquid methane, and the second largest.

  • Uranus

    50,724 KM

    A cold ice giant that rotates on its side, likely due to a past collision. Its atmosphere is rich in methane, giving it a blue color.

  • Neptune

    49,244 KM

    The farthest planet from the Sun, known for its deep blue color and the strongest winds in the Solar System.
